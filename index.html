Bio

I am an Associate Professor at Suleyman Demirel University whose research lies at the intersection of transportation engineering, intelligent transportation systems, and AI-based infrastructure analytics. My work focuses on traffic safety, driver behavior, traffic operations, and predictive modeling for transportation and pavement systems. Using machine learning, simulation, and statistical analysis, I develop applied methods that support safer, more efficient, and more resilient mobility and infrastructure systems. I am particularly interested in international research collaboration, interdisciplinary projects, and research opportunities involving smart mobility, predictive infrastructure, and data-driven engineering.

Research Focus

Intelligent Transportation Systems

Data-driven and technology-enabled approaches for improving mobility, traffic management, and system performance.

Analysis of driver behavior, operational risk, and safety-focused interventions for more reliable road systems.

AI for Transportation Engineering

Application of machine learning, simulation, and predictive analytics to transportation and infrastructure problems.

Predictive Infrastructure Analytics

Modeling pavement and infrastructure performance to support maintenance planning, resilience, and decision-making.

Simulation, Optimization & Decision Support

Development of analytical and computational tools for evaluating transport operations and engineering strategies.
